Exploring New Zealand on the Map

Welcome to New Zealand. Click on the map to view the detailed route

New Zealand is also known as Otahitora (in Māori, meaning "land of long white clouds"). It is a sovereign island country located in the southwest of the Pacific Ocean and is a major component of the New Zealand Federation. Its capital is Wellington, and the largest city is Auckland. New Zealand is mainly composed of two islands: the North Island (Te Ika-a-Maui) and the South Island (Te Waipounamu). These two islands are separated by Cook Strait. The capital Wellington is located at the southern end of the North Island, and the capital also includes some small islands. New Zealand is the sixth-largest island country in the world. New Zealand faces Australia across the Tasman Sea, about 1,500 kilometers from the east coast of Australia. It is about 1,000 kilometers away from the South Pacific islands of New Caledonia, East Timor, and Fiji. Its unique geographical location makes New Zealand one of the last regions discovered and settled by humans. The country has rich tourist attractions and is famous for its pristine natural environment. Due to its long isolation from the outside world, it has formed a unique and extremely rich ecosystem.
